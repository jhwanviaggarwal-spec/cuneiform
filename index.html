Origins of Cuneiform

Cuneiform is one of the earliest writing systems, developed by Sumerians around 3200 BCE. Wedge-shaped marks were pressed into clay tablets to record laws, trade, and literature.

Fun Fact: Cuneiform is the foundation of written human history.

Preserving the Epic of Gilgamesh

The Epic of Gilgamesh was composed in Sumerian and later in Akkadian on clay tablets. Thanks to cuneiform, the adventures of Gilgamesh and Enkidu survived thousands of years.

Legacy Through Writing

Cuneiform reflects the desire for lasting legacy. Gilgamesh sought immortality, but his stories on clay tablets preserved his memory for millennia.

Clay tablets act as bridges between the ancient world and the present, keeping human memory alive.

Culture and Knowledge

Beyond literature, cuneiform was used for laws, trade, and religious texts. It allowed Mesopotamians to structure society, transmit knowledge, and maintain cultural continuity.

Insight: Written records preserved human achievements beyond the limits of life.
